Who we are

Ultrasound Africa is a voluntary provider of Continuous Professional Development programmes and training in ultrasound to members of professional bodies and teaching institutions that are involved in the field of Radiology and Imaging. Our CPD sessions and training sessions have impacted thousands of ultrasound practitioners since 2017.

Our ultrasound research and outreach programmes are aimed at supporting quality healthcare delivery and informing policy in mainly resource-limited settings in Africa. The idea of supporting good quality healthcare delivery in low and middle-income countries was birthed in 2017 by ultrasound practitioners in high-income settings but with a background of low resource settings. We train already qualified ultrasound practitioners in both basic and specialty sonography techniques and offer high-quality outreach ultrasound services voluntarily to low resource settings.

Vision

Our Vision is to be the premier CPD provider and support high-quality ultrasound Education and Research

Mission

Our Mission is to deliver professional development to members of professional ultrasound bodies and ultrasound teaching institutions through high-quality ultrasound education and research

Team Players

Geoffrey Tugume Benjamin

Team Lead

B G Tugume is an enthusiastic Ultrasound Practitioner based at North Middlesex University Hospital London in the United Kingdom and is a Clinical Supervisor and Educator on the Kings College London Postgraduate Ultrasound Programme. He is also on the ultrasound academic faculty Team at Ernest Cook Ultrasound Research and Education Institute, Kampala, Uganda. He is the founding President of UltrasoundCPD Education, an organisation that was renamed Ultrasound Africa. He holds a Master of Science in Diagnostic Ultrasound and two PG. Diplomas in Diagnostic Ultrasound. He pioneered Bone Ultrasound Imaging and Lung Ultrasound in Uganda in 2006 and holds annual Lung Ultrasound and Fetal Cardiac Sonography seminars and practical clinic sessions in Uganda for ultrasound practitioners including sonographers, radiologists, chest physicians and obstetricians. He is passionate about delivery of good quality diagnostic ultrasound services in resource limited settings. He is also the founder and CEO of The Dearkids Family, an organization based in Uganda that helps support and maintain underprivileged children in school.

Samuel Lincoln Bukenya

Director Education and  Training

Lincoln Bukenya is a specialist musculoskeletal sonographer and lecturer at Birmingham City University, United Kingdom. His key areas of interest include ultrasound imaging in inflammatory arthropathy, trauma and soft tissue tumour imaging. He is also interested in advancement of ultrasound training, research and innovation.


Theonille Mukabagorora

Director Research and Development

Theonille is a lecturer at the University of Rwanda since 2016 and is currently a PhD candidate at the University of Pretoria with a special interest in vascular sonography. She obtained a PG Diploma in Teaching and Learning in Higher Institutions from the University of Rwanda, College of Education, in 2019. She obtained her Masters Degree in Diagnostic Sonography in 2017 from Ernest Cook Ultrasound Research and Education Institute ( ECUREI), Uganda. Theonille is a member of the Medical Imaging Society of Rwanda where she chairs the Education and Research committee since 2019. Her clinical work started in 2009 at Butare Teaching Hospital Rwanda where she started as a radiographer. However, since 2010, she has oriented her interest towards Diagnostic Sonography.



Vitus Akashemererwa

Director Accreditation, Guidelines and Protocols

Vitus oversees the development of ultrasound Protocols, guidelines and the accreditation process. He holds a Msc Degree in Diagnostic Ultrasound, a BSc in Medical Imaging and PG Cert in Echocardiography. He has special interest in Sports medicine and physical health diagnostics (Ultrasound and MRI), MSK sonography, Cardiovascular MRI & General Sonography, Neonatal Neuro-sonography, Ocular and vascular Ultrasound. He has done Research in Ocular Doppler in diabetes and hypertension and Abdominal ultrasound in the paediatric emergency. Vitus is currently the Head of the Imaging and Diagnostics Department at Nerves and Bones Rehabilitation Centre, Kampala Uganda.Vitus is also the Ultrasound Coordinator & Trainer of Trainees, iTECH (Stillbirths in high burden setting: Ample room for improvement) Research Project Makerere University Hospital since 2022.

Edwin Ruhinda

Director of Operations

Edwin Ruhinda is a highly skilled and experienced fetal medicine specialist sonographer, with over fifteen years of experience in the field of ultrasound imaging. He also possesses extensive knowledge in extracorporeal shockwave lithotripsy treatment. In addition to his clinical work, Edwin is deeply passionate about teaching and has supervised numerous postgraduate ultrasound students throughout his career. He is a member of the faculty for the ultrasound course by RCOG-approved NCEL deanery, where he shares his extensive knowledge and expertise with junior doctors.

Aminah Nabisere Nakato

She has a MSc degree in Diagnostic Ultrasound and is also the CPD coordinator and Treasurer for the Society of Radiographers Uganda.
